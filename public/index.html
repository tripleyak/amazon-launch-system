The Product

One sphere. No seam.

Injection-Molded · Typical Competitor

The failure surface is a line.

Injection molding joins two hemispheres at a seam. Under impact, that seam is the weakest point on the ball — and pickleball is played at 60 mph off a paddle. The crack you see above is not cosmetic; it changes flight, bounce, and spin. It's the single most-cited defect in the category.

Rotomolding is different. Heated polymer rotates inside a mold until it cools into one uniform wall. No seam. No weak line. Just a ball that stays round.
